My name is Murdo Maclachlan. It is pronounced: /'məɹ.dəʊ mak.'lax.lan/
I have a middle name, Bathgate, but that makes my name quite long, so when I use it, I normally contract it to Murdo B. Maclachlan.
Murdo is a Gaelic name of uncertain origin. It is an anglicisation of Murchadh, which is variably seen as deriving from the Irish muir ('sea') and cadh ('battle'), or the Scottish Gaelic name Muireadhach, which means 'lord' but possibly also derives from muir. See sources and more information on Wikipedia.
Bathgate comes from a town in West Lothian, Scotland. It derives from either the Scottish Gaelic, both chèit, or the Cumbric, badd + ceto-, both meaning 'boar wood'. See sources and more information on Wikipedia and Wiktionary.
Maclachlan is the name of a Scottish clan. It means 'Son of Lachlann'. Lachlann is a Gaelic name deriving from Lochlann, which means 'land of lochs', 'loch' being the Scottish term for 'lake'. This name was used to denote people from Scandinavia, or specifically, Norway. See sources and more information on Wikipedia: Maclachaln, Lachlann, and Lochlann.
There are many forms of the name 'Maclachlan', with spelling, capitalisation and pronunciation varying greatly. My name is spelt and pronounced only as documented here.
