Transcribing

Transcribing? What's that? Transcribing is accessibility, is what it is! This is something I spend a good portion of my time doing, and I've met many wonderful people doing it too, so it's very close to my heart. But what is it really? It's not your normal transcribing initiative, such as a large library organising an effort to transcribe old documents; no, at the moment it's something quite a lot smaller. I'd like to introduce you to the Grafeas Group (logo right), a small 501(c)(3) non-profit organisation that runs r/TranscribersOfReddit, an accessibility initiative to transcribe audio, image and video posts on Reddit for the benefit of people who would not normally have access to them. This lack of access can be due to visual or hearing impairents, slow internet, inconsistency of rendering across Reddit's various official and third-party clients, and more. To help these people, the posts are transcribed into the comments, where they can be accessed either by simply reading, or where necessary, by assisstive technology such as screen readers. I've been transcribing with the Grafeas Group for over a year as of July 2021, and helping moderate the subreddit since February 2021. I've even used a few transcriptions myself in the past when the contrast between the text and the background of images didn't suit my colour vision (I have protanomaly). I could talk for hours about transcribing and the Grafeas Group, but it's probably already sounding like an advert. Biased as I may be, I do genuinely believe they're one of the best communities on the internet!

Writing

Over time I've written more first chapters than you might think, and I believe beginnings have become for me the easiest part of a story. I have many stories with a rough plan, and one that I've written through several full drafts of. The hardest part for me these days is deciding what to write first. The novel I've fully drafted, a high-fantasy novel tentatively titled A Daemon's Influence, is not currently my main focus. That may be surprising, considering it's the only thing I've written to completion thus far, but I've discovered it will take a lot more careful planning to make as good as it can be, and so in the mean time I decided to work on something shorter. After realising that A Daemon's Influence was not going to be the first prose work I published, I moved on to thinking about other ideas. I've written several chapters of a gothic low-fantasy novel entitled A Lady in Black, and have the majority of my plans for the low-fantasy steampunk stories, The Faces of Undertown and The Iron City written out, but in August 2021 an idea occurred to me that I didn't want to let slip away. And thus, the apocalyptic far-future science fiction novella Infinity's Omega was born. Infinity's Omega is the first science fiction prose I've ever written, as well as the first novella. I'm in the late stages of the first draft currently, and I've found that the shorter story format allowed for much tighter writing and an easier time plotting out a full plan before even setting the firt word to paper. In terms of George R.R. Martin's metaphor of the Two Types of Writer, I suppose this makes me a full architect just this once, where normally I'm an archtitectural gardener. I hoped to finish the first draft of Infinity's Omega before returning to university for my second year, but I've since seen this won't be possible. That's partially my fault for spontaneously deciding to write a website, but hey! I wanted to. I'll still be finishing the draft soon anyway, and that's what really matters.
